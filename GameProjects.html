Release Date: April 2022

During my third year of university I took a course which had my and a group of 14 other students would work together and use our experience to try and create a project. Working on a full year game allowed us to learn about some usefull techniques to both organize everyones times and keep everyone updated with progress on what everyone worked on. As the technical/unity lead for the project I had decided to organized the technical peoples jobs into a general jobs that would allow us to keep us somewhat organized was by giving certain people general roles that they could fufill. So we had one person mostly in charge of UI while another one might be encharge of replacing all current assets with the art teams assets. This meant that for the most part people would generally stay in their own areas of code which would reduce the chances that the same set of code might be affected. Though as we didnt have a lot of experience in making sure we had a standard programming practices in place for most of the project, we were small enough of a team that we could slowly learn how to better keep the code to a general standard that helped with redability.

During the project we also decided to have weekly agail meetings where everyone talked about the things that they worked on for the past week. This allowed everyone to have a rough idea on the progress being made on diffrent components for the game and in turn even surface any problems that people might be having when they arent progressing through

Mining safty prototype (First internship)

As part of a 4-month internship in Peru with a mining company in 2021, I worked on a prototype safety project which was meant to test out how possible it might be to make a full unity project that would be used to train and test workers on mine safty protocol. This project was made as part of a larger chain reserch on new training systems that the mining company was intreested in implementing and wanted to investigate the diffrent challenges that the diffrent systems might incurre.

Throughout the project, I was able to quickly learn some of the best ways to appropriatly document all of the diffrent things I was working on so that anyone in the future could more easily understand what I was had been working on. In addition, as the mining company had a more corporate culture with people that might have a drastict level of techincal experience I had to learn quickly how to explain the typical process of making a project like this using a game engine and other similar systems.
