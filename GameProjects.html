AI learning project (In the works)

Throughout my time at Brock university, I became more interested in AI systems in games and how well-implemented AI allows the rest of the game features to shine even better. This meant that when I learned about a semester-long course that allowed me to work on any project, I wanted that would expand my skill set, I decided to take it and focus on designing and programming as interesting AI systems as possible.

Before the class started, I decided to research standard AI systems over the years and how they were usually implemented in different games. As I studied for the project, I had initially planned on making a Finite State Machine (FSM) and behavior tree systems in Unreal Engine 5 to get as much experience as possible. But after a few weeks of trying to learn Unreal, I decided to use Unity instead and focus on making the AI system with an FSM. This would give me enough time to make sure that the AI was unique and give them enough different behaviors so that when people played against them, they would have a somewhat challenging opponent while also making them seem like they were thinking like "real people."

While working on the AI systems, I realized I needed to implement a Director AI for combat to improve their decision-making and make them seem like they were making decisions like actual human beings. The main parts connected to the DIrector AI were the cover and attack states which would occur when an AI detected the player and needed to decide whether to attack or not. The initial problem I was trying to solve was how to create difficulty in combat between the player and the AIs while not overwhelming the player by having all enemies attack the player simultaneously. I decided to solve this by having each enemy that detects or is alerted of the player's nearby presence enter an "Aggression" state in which they would calculate a value based on specific information from its environment it knows about, with a higher value meaning that the AI is more willing to attack the player then go to cover. At this point, it will save the weight and request the Director AI to state that it would like to attack the player.

Mining safty prototype (First internship)

As part of a 4-month internship in Peru with a mining company in 2021, I worked on a prototype safety project which was meant to test out how possible it might be to make a full unity project that would be used to train and test workers on mine safty protocol. This project was made as part of a larger chain reserch on new training systems that the mining company was intreested in implementing and wanted to investigate the diffrent challenges that the diffrent systems might incurre.

Throughout the project, I was able to quickly learn some of the best ways to appropriatly document all of the diffrent things I was working on so that anyone in the future could more easily understand what I was had been working on. In addition, as the mining company had a more corporate culture with people that might have a drastict level of techincal experience I had to learn quickly how to explain the typical process of making a project like this using a game engine and other similar systems.
